Department of Computer Science 

Most of the faculty and students who work in the area of computer architecture and compilers are affiliated with the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering (located in Everitt Laboratory) or the Department of Computer Science (located in the Siebel Center for Computer Science). We have additional office locations in the Coordinated Science Lab and Beckman Institute.

Our computing facilities are state-of-the-art, including a new computing cluster that consists of 75 computers with two processors each. Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) generously donated the 150 Athlon MP 2000+ processors that power this cluster. The rest of the funding for the cluster was made possible with the help of a $120,000 grant from the National Science Foundation along with cost sharing funds from the College of Engineering, the Office of the Vice Chancellor of Research, the Department of Computer Science, and the Coordinated Science Lab.
