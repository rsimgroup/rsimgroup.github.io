The RSIM Computer Architecture Group in the Computer Science Department at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign explores emerging research areas in computer architecture and performance evaluation models.

Much of the work in the group is targeted at emerging application suites, including multimedia, communication, and network applications.

The center of the group's focus is on adaptive architectures for power and performance. Current work also includes thermal modeling, exploring the design space between chip multi-processors (CMPs) and simultaneous multithreaded processors (SMTs), and real time scheduling in multithreaded architectures.

Other contributions are in the areas of exploiting instruction level parallelism or ILP for memory performance in uniprocessors and multiprocessors, evaluation techniques for systems with ILP processors, and memory consistency models for shared-memory multiprocessors.
